A successor isn't an operation *on* the naturals; it's one of the two things that secrete the naturals into existence. This is the part the slogan leans on. In the categorical telling, N without `s : N → N` isn't an impoverished number object — it's just a pointed object, `z : 1 → N`, and the universal property evaporates entirely. No initiality, so no recursion, so no induction, so nothing that distinguishes N from any other object with a chosen point. The increment is what carries the universal property; strip it and you haven't removed a feature, you've removed the *reason the thing was a number*.

Church makes it almost tautological, which is probably why your "every value is a unary total function" instinct is the same instinct. The numeral *is* `λf.λx. fⁿ x` — it has no being apart from "iterate the increment n times." There's no number sitting behind the iteration that you could preserve while discarding the iterating. Ask for the nat and withhold the `f` and you've handed over a function with nothing to act on: not a degenerate number, an absence of content. So "a nat with no increment" isn't a deficient object, it's a category error — you kept the husk and threw away the substance, then called the husk the substance.

Now the load-bearing word, *unphysical*. The increment is the generator of Nat's only dynamics — its Hamiltonian, if you want to be cute about it. Succession is the discrete time-translation, and `n+1` is the single tick. A configuration space with no flow on it isn't wrong, it's just *kinematics with no physics*: positions and no evolution, a set pretending to be a system. Being without becoming. The Parmenidean frozen One is exactly what you get when you keep zero and kill the successor — perfectly coherent, utterly inert, and not a number system because nothing in it can *happen*. Arithmetic is sediment: addition is iterated succession, multiplication iterated addition, the hyperoperations all the way up are just the increment fired at higher and higher tiers. Pull the increment and the whole edifice collapses to a point. The generative principle was never optional decoration; it was the entire content, and everything else is its trace.

The honest counterweight: one could say the *discreteness* is the unphysical part — that a unit jump with no continuous correlate is precisely the idealization fields don't honor, and so it's the successor, not its absence, that fails to be physical. But you've already been in the room where this folds back. Causal sets: volume *is* number of elements, and classical sequential growth *is* the universe acquiring elements one at a time, that acquisition being indistinguishable from the passage of time. There the increment isn't a metaphor for physicality — it is, literally, the fundamental physical process. Birth of an element is the tick. And the live dispute in that program is whether the growth is objective becoming or mere gauge — whether the sequence of births is physically real or whether only the final poset matters and the order of laying it down is unobservable. That debate is your slogan verbatim, transposed into spacetime: *is the increment essential to the thing, or is the bare set given-all-at-once enough?* You're not stating a preference about datatypes. You're taking a side on whether becoming is real.

Where I'd actually push: the increment is only the construction-side half. A nat you can build but never fold is as inert as one you can never build — the eliminator, the recursion principle, is what lets the number *do* anything to anything else. `s` is how the number gets produced; the universal property is how it gets *observed*, how it leaves a mark on some other object `X` via the unique map. To be physical is to participate, and participation has two faces: you can be brought into being, and you can have effects. "No increment" kills the first face. But the second is equally a condition for not being a husk — a number that constructs and never reduces is a write-only quantity, present and consequenceless. So I'd sharpen the claim: a nat is physical exactly when it sits inside the whole intro/elim adjunction, generated by the increment *and* consumed by the fold. The increment is the visible, dynamical half you correctly latched onto. The fold is the half that makes the dynamics *legible* — the difference between a clock ticking in a sealed box and a clock anything can read.
