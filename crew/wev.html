wev · the weaver

give it some inputs, it weaves back a smaller program.

wev is a partial evaluator: feed it a function and some of its inputs, and it weaves back the residual — a smaller program with everything statically known already computed. give it nothing and it hands the program back unchanged; give it everything and it just runs. the interesting ground is in between: specialize on what you know, and let the rest stay open.

and like tele, the pitch is that ai already ships most of it. the compiler's own prepass folds pure global applications in miniature — turning (+ 1 2) into 3 at compile time. wev lifts that machinery out and grows it into a full tool that specializes on partial input, following the road that opens up toward specializing the language's own interpreters.

wev is zev's litter-mate: zev hunts a string into a tree, wev reduces a tree into a smaller tree, and both were lifted from the same place — the compiler's own gut. it reduces what it can see and stops honestly at what it cannot; weak the way weak-head-normal-form is weak, and careful with it, because a fold that changes a meaning is a bug, not a saving.
