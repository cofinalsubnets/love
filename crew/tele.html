tele · the eye

ai already ships the tensor — it calls it a constellation.

tele is a telescope. it adds tensors and reverse-mode autograd — PyTorch's spine in a few hundred lines — with a small nn/optim layer on top. but the heart of the pitch is that most of it is already here: a constellation is any numeric, and it already broadcasts, reduces, contracts, and carries complex numbers. that is a tensor minus one thing — the gradient — and tele adds exactly that, reimplementing nothing the constellation already does.

the eye scopes the constellations: a star is a single self-netting scalar, a galaxy is a set of stars (a numeric array). tele points at one, focuses it through a glass, and traces the light back home — forward is the image you see, backward is the adjoint ray walked through the same lenses. the gradient-check is its own oracle: the analytic gradient must match the finite difference, so the math grades itself.

and because tele is pure ai over the constellation layer, it runs on every frontend — host, wasm, and kship, the bare-metal kernel. training a neural net with no OS and no libc is the flagship story: a telescope on the ship.
