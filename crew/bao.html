bao · the shell

a soft wrap around a chewy command.

bao is a steamed bun — a soft wrapper around a chewy command. it is three things that turn out to be one. it is the interactive shell: line editing, history, a prompt, and a fault-face that survives every raise. it is the rlwrap: a pty wrapper that lends any program bao's own editor. and it is the debugger: a help handler with a face, built straight on the condition system.

all three are the same muscle — one editor, a wrapped process, and the condition machinery — so they share one tool. write the line editor once, then reuse it everywhere: typing at the prompt, feeding a wrapped child, inspecting a caught scare. one bao, three hats.

bao keeps the surface small and the editor private — only the few entry points the shell really needs are global; the rest lives tucked inside one closure, off the bag. a soft outside, a deliberate inside. bao is aineko's sibling — the cat pumps bytes over a socket, bao pumps them through a pty — and its fault-face is kin to kship's watchdog: both are a help handler that keeps its head when something goes wrong.

lives in ai/bao.l + host/pty.c · the full brief is crew/bao.md
