cook · the builder

make, in ai.

cook is make — written in ai. it reads a Makefile (or its own Cookfile), resolves the recipes, and runs them; cook --emit transpiles a Makefile into a flat, fully-resolved Cookfile with every variable and wildcard already worked out. it is make-compatible enough to build the host from scratch and pass the corpus — the language building itself, with its own tool.

that is the quiet trick of the whole crew: cook is written in the very language it compiles into being. it reads the same Makefiles you already have, but the engine underneath is ai closures over the same tiny core every other crew member rides — no separate build runtime, no second language to learn.

cook has the lightest coupling aboard: pure ai over the shared command line, so it needs no new C and no special wiring to do its work. it just reads the recipe and gets on with it.
