kship · the ship

the language, perceiving, deciding, acting on bare metal.

kship is the ship the whole crew rides. it is the freestanding ai kernel grown a network stack and a persistent agent loop: it perceives (the network and the clock), decides (a policy — that is ai at the helm), and acts (the wire and spawned tasks), unattended, on real hardware. no Linux, no libc — it boots off a laptop and runs.

it is not a greenfield OS. the substrate was mostly already here — the egg, the scheduler, the condition system, the wait-on-many-sources machinery — so kship is the kernel grown a NIC and an agent loop, the ship that sails a sea the core already laid down. the perceive → decide → act fold never returns; it just keeps going, one decision after the next, forward, like the VM beneath it.

the others are the ship's systems — aineko the net, bao the shell, tele the eye, siri the cartographer of names — and kship is the hull that carries them all. a watchdog with a face keeps it on course: a fault doesn't sink the ship, it welps and the loop carries the last good state forward. that is what makes it self-driving.
