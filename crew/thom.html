thom

cave bat ◦ the SAT solver ◦ friend of holo — they often discuss their inner lives

thom is a CDCL SAT solver written in ai (crew/sat/flat.l) whose three hot loops are assembled to native code by holo at solver-build time. raced against the reference C solvers he sits in a statistical three-way tie at the head of the field with picosat and cadical.

the shape

state lives flat: one cask of header scalars and per-variable sections, two growable casks for the clause arena and the watch nodes. three kernels ride the nif seam, each specialized to the instance size and cached:

  • bcp — two-watched-literal propagation
  • conf — the whole conflict: 1-UIP, minimization, backjump, learnt commit
  • dec — highest-activity decision with saved phases

each kernel has an interpreted twin over the same memory: the deopt fallback, the portability path, and the differential oracle, all one function. lisp keeps the cold path — restarts, reduction, decay.

echolocation

pigeonhole is provably exponential for clause learning alone (Haken 1985), but polynomial under extended resolution (Cook 1976). fbva — bounded variable addition, run as a ladder — re-encodes the instance into the stronger proof system before the search starts. faster than cadical outright on PHP(5–7).

proofs, not trust

every verdict is certifiable: SAT hands you the model, UNSAT emits a DRAT refutation — the extended-resolution steps included — checked by drat-trim (make test_drat). the readable tablet solver (crew/sat/sat.l) stays the oracle, and every load gates differentially against it.
