aineko · the cat

bytes pump both ways.

aineko is the cat — 愛猫, beloved cat — a netcat clone in about fifty lines of ai. aineko host port dials out, aineko -l port listens; once the line is up, bytes pump both ways between the socket and stdin/stdout, with DNS to find the far end. a tiny, useful thing that does exactly one job.

the cat is the crew's real apps day one demo — the first proof that ai does real host I/O, not just clever arithmetic. and a bidirectional pump is exactly the shape ai's cooperative scheduler was built for: two spawned pump loops, each parked on its own socket, wake the moment their fd is ready, with no select loop in sight.

aineko is also the shared trunk — the pump-and-teardown discipline it got right is the same one bao hangs its pty wrapper off, and kship its bare-metal net stack. land the cat cleanly and two other crew members stand on it. it hunts the net; sift, its housemate, cleans up after.

lives in host/net.c + tools/aineko.l · the full brief is crew/aineko.md
