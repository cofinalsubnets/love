holo

cave spider ◦ the assembler ◦ short for holophrasm

holo is a tiny assembler written in ai (crew/holo/): a neutral, RISC-ish IR lowered to machine bytes by pluggable backends — x86-64 and aarch64 today. she is baked into the binary as the holo book, and her web holds the whole native story: the JIT is her client, and so is thom.

the shape

the IR is a flat list of instruction forms — a three-address surface over abstract registers r0..r14 + sp. holo.l is the arch-neutral half: the two-pass layout (assign addresses, resolve label fixups) and the backend registry. a backend is one {lower, patch} pair pinned under a target name — x64.l and arm64.l ride it, a third drops in the same way. every arm64 word was checked against llvm-mc.

the web

  • the glaze — the template JIT compiles hot loops through holo's IR
  • thom — the three SAT kernels, assembled at solver-build time
  • elf.l — a minimal static linker: wrap the bytes in an ELF, chmod +x, run
  • demo.l — two runnable binaries, the proof the bytes are real machine code
